# Actions principle

Fermat's principle & Maupertuis :
$$S = mvr = pr$$

Lagrange :
$$S(q) = \int_{t_i}^{t_f} L(q,\dot q,t)\ dt$$

$$\Delta S = 0$$

A comprehensive consideration of the change in energy (state) and the time of occurrence; that is, a **comprehensive consideration of the change in momentum and distance.**
## d’Alembert、Hamilton
$$\lim_{\Delta\to0}\Delta s = ds$$
## Lagrange
$$S(q) = \int_{t_i}^{t_f} (\frac{\partial L}{\partial q}\partial q+\frac{\partial L}{\partial\dot q}\partial\dot q)\ dt$$
## Hamiltonian
$$S = \int_{t_i}^{t_f} (p\dot q-H)\ dt$$
## Hamilton–Jacobi
$$H(q,\frac{\partial S}{\partial q},t)+\frac{\partial S}{\partial t} = 0$$
