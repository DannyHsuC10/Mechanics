# Energy & Coefficient of restitution

$$e = \frac{E_f}{E_i}$$

## kinetic energy

$$K' = \frac12m'v^2$$

$$e = \frac{v_{sys\ f}}{v_{sys\ i}}$$

$$K_{sys\ i}' = \frac12m_{sys}'v_{sys\ i}^2$$

$$K_{sys\ f}' = \frac12m_{sys}'v_{sys\ f}^2 = \frac12m_{sys}'(ev_{sys\ i})^2 = \frac12e^2m_{sys}'v_{sys\ i}^2$$

$$K_{sys\ f}' = K_{sys\ i}'e^2$$

$$e^2 = \frac{K_{sys\ f}}{K_{sys\ i}}$$

$$e = \sqrt{\frac{K_{sys\ f}}{K_{sys\ i}}}$$

## potential energy
Hitting the wall $F = ma\ \ (m \to  \infty,\ a \to 0)$

$$P_i = P_f$$

$$e = \frac{v_{f}}{v_{i}}$$

1. $P_i\to K_i$

$$mgh_i = \frac12mv_i^2$$

$$v_i = \sqrt{2gh_i}$$

2. $K_i\to K_f$

$$v_f = ev_i = e\sqrt{2gh_i}$$

3. $K_f\to P_f$

$$\frac12mv_f^2 = mgh_f$$

$$\frac12m(e\sqrt{2gh_i})^2 = mgh_f$$

$$m(e\sqrt{2gh_i})^2 = m2gh_f$$

$$2mgh_ie^2 = 2mgh_f$$

$$mgh_ie^2 = mgh_f$$

$$e^2 = \frac{P_f}{P_i}$$

$$e = \sqrt{\frac{P_f}{P_i}}$$
